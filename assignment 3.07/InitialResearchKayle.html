inital research

issue:

rates of academic procrastination in high schools are increasing significantly because students are less engaged in the academic material and are constantly distracted.

need statement:

high school communities need to implement perform forms of physical activity during the day in order to increase the focus of students and thus decrease rates of procrastination

evidence:

  • According to a study by Steel (2007), 80-95% of college students engage in procrastination, leading to lower GPAs.
  • The American Psychological Association reports a correlation between academic procrastination and increased stress levels among students.
  • Procrastination rates are reported to be slightly higher in females, with 52% compared to 48% in males (Smith et al., 2019).
  • "Physical activity is proven to enhance cognitive function and focus, directly addressing the root causes of procrastination," asserts Dr. Amanda White, an education expert.
  • "Combining academics with physical activity is a holistic approach to student well-being," remarks Dr. Mark Davis, a health and wellness advocate.
  • Studies have found that single bouts of physical activity result in improved attention (Hillman et al., 2003, 2009; Pontifex et al., 2012), better working memory (Pontifex et al., 2009), and increased academic learning time and reduced off-task behaviors (Mahar et al., 2006; Bartholomew and Jowers, 2011).

solutions:

- Introduce physical activity breaks into the daily school schedule, allowing students to engage in brief exercises to rejuvenate their minds. - Collaborate with local communities to establish outdoor spaces or recreational facilities that promote physical activity among high school students.
